🌸 Categorized Activities & Interests

I believe in pursuing what truly inspires me. Here is how I divide my core activities, creative pursuits, and media interests. You can also explore my code repositories directly on my GitHub profile page to see my work.

🎮 Game Dev & Modding

  • Visual Novel / RPG Hybrids: My primary interest is to become a developer creating immersive VN/RPG hybrid games with rich branching narratives.
  • Engine Customization: Tinkering with retro engine files and writing custom styling code to adjust UI configurations.

🦇 Retro Horror Gaming & Niche Movies

  • Atmospheric Classics: I love playing older, highly atmospheric survival horror video games like the original Silent Hill 2 and Obscure.
  • Janky Gems: I have a special place in my heart for quirky horror games that aren't technically polished or feature cheesy voice acting, like Deadly Premonition and Tormented Souls.
  • Niche Horror Movies: Exploring hidden indie horror gems and retro psychological thrillers.

🖤 Anime & Visual Kei Music

  • Favorite Series: I live for stylish and dark anime like JoJo's Bizarre Adventure (JJBA), Banana Fish, and Devilman Crybaby.
  • Visual Kei (VK): I am a huge fan of VK music, appreciating its unique fusion of theatrical rock, gothic fashion, and expressive melodies. My personal playlist spans over 1,200 tracks and includes bands like lynch., DADAROMA, JILUKA, Versailles, DIAURA, and DazzlingBAD.

🌸 My Top 10 Lists

To give you a better idea of the specific niches I live for, here are my separate top ten lists for anime, survival horror video games, and psychological/indie horror movies:

🎬 Top 10 Favorite Anime Series & Movies

  1. Banana Fish — An absolute masterpiece street thriller with profound, deeply moving character bonds.
  2. JoJo's Bizarre Adventure — Stylized, theatrical action with iconic poses and musical references.
  3. Devilman Crybaby — A hyper-stylized, dark, tragic, and visually breathtaking modern masterpiece.
  4. Serial Experiments Lain — Deep cyber-psychology, digital dissociation, and dark philosophical atmosphere.
  5. Jujutsu Kaisen — Dynamic battles, dark occult themes, and stunning animations.
  6. Perfect Blue — Satoshi Kon's legendary psychological dread masterpiece mapping identity and obsession.
  7. Neon Genesis Evangelion — Masterful psychological struggles and iconic retro-mecha design.
  8. Mononoke — Avant-garde horror-fantasy with gorgeous theatrical art style.
  9. Texhnolyze — Gritty, bleak cyberpunk with intense existential dread.
  10. Monster — A dark, realistic psychological thriller about morality, guilt, and obsession.

🎮 Top 10 Favorite Horror Video Games

  1. Silent Hill 2 (OG) — The absolute peak of psychological horror and melancholic atmosphere.
  2. Obscure — Nostalgic, co-op survival horror with a distinct high-school slasher vibe.
  3. Deadly Premonition — Quirky, janky Twin Peaks-inspired gem with legendary cheesy acting.
  4. Tormented Souls — Fantastic modern callback to classic fixed-camera survival horror.
  5. Silent Hill 3 — Terrifying industrial decay aesthetics and stellar sound design.
  6. Rule of Rose — Cruel, psychological gothic narrative with incredibly eerie atmosphere.
  7. Fatal Frame II: Crimson Butterfly — Haunting Japanese folklore horror and camera-based gameplay.
  8. Siren (Forbidden Siren) — Highly atmospheric, challenging, multi-perspective stealth horror.
  9. Kuon — Rare Heian-period Japanese survival horror by FromSoftware.
  10. Cry of Fear — Gritty, psychological source-engine mod with terrifying claustrophobia.

📼 Top 10 Favorite Horror & Psychological Movies

  1. [REC] (2007) — Intense, claustrophobic Spanish mockumentary that is one of the best found-footage films ever made.
  2. Lake Mungo (2008) — A haunting, melancholic mockumentary about grief, ghosts, and existential dread.
  3. Martyrs (2008) — An extremely heavy, thought-provoking French transgressive horror masterpiece on pain and transcendence.
  4. Perfect Blue (1997) — An unforgettable psychological thriller exploring identity, dread, and blurred reality.
  5. The Borderlands (2013) — Also known as Final Prayer, a slow-burn British found-footage film with a deeply disturbing ending.
  6. Grave Encounters (2011) — A fun, claustrophobic mockumentary exploring a haunted psychiatric hospital that warps space and time.
  7. Picnic (1996) — Shunji Iwai's short, dark, and beautifully whimsical psychological film about three patients walking on walls.
  8. Pulse (Kairo - 2001) — Kiyoshi Kurosawa's ultimate depiction of internet-dread and loneliness.
  9. Noroi: The Curse (2005) — A sprawling, deeply unsettling found-footage J-horror mystery.
  10. Cure (1997) — A hypnotic, philosophical J-horror mystery about suggestion, memory, and murder.
