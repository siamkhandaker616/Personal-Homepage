🌸 Categorized Activities & Interests

I believe in pursuing what truly inspires me. Here is how I divide my core activities, creative pursuits, and media interests. You can also explore my code repositories directly on my GitHub profile page to see my work.

🎮 Game Dev & Modding

  • Visual Novel / RPG Hybrids: My primary interest is to become a developer creating immersive VN/RPG hybrid games with rich branching narratives.
  • Engine Customization: Tinkering with retro engine files and writing custom styling code to adjust UI configurations.

🦇 Retro Horror Gaming & Niche Movies

  • Atmospheric Classics: I love playing older, highly atmospheric survival horror video games like the original Silent Hill 2 and Obscure.

  • Favorite Series: I live for stylish and dark anime like JoJo's Bizarre Adventure (JJBA), Banana Fish, and Devilman Crybaby.

  9. Texhnolyze — Gritty, bleak cyberpunk with intense existential dread.
  10. Monster — A dark, realistic psychological thriller about morality, guilt, and obsession.

🎮 My Horror Video Games Recommendation List

Category / Era Game Title Release Details Core Aesthetic & Description (Multi-line)
Classic PS2 Era
(Golden Age) 		Silent Hill 2 (OG) 2001 / Konami The absolute peak of psychological horror and melancholic atmosphere. It explores heavy themes of guilt, grief, and punishment, set against the backdrop of a foggy town that manifests the protagonist's deepest traumas into physical monstrosities.
Silent Hill 3 2003 / Konami A terrifying industrial decay masterpiece with stellar sound design by Akira Yamaoka. It features intense physiological horror and psychological tension, following a teenager's surreal journey through rust, blood, and cult symbols.
Rule of Rose 2006 / Sony A cruel, psychological gothic narrative set in a surreal orphanage ruled by children. It is widely known for its hauntingly beautiful soundtrack, highly disturbing themes, and incredibly eerie, dream-like fairy tale atmosphere.
Fatal Frame II 2003 / Tecmo Also known as Crimson Butterfly, this haunting J-horror classic features a camera-based combat system. It follows twin sisters lost in an abandoned village overrun by malevolent spirits, drawing on deep Japanese folklore.
Siren (Forbidden) 2003 / Sony A highly atmospheric, challenging, multi-perspective stealth horror game. It requires players to 'sightjack' (see through the eyes of) the undead shibito to survive a perpetual red rain curse in a rural village.
Modern Retro &
Indie Gems 		Tormented Souls 2021 / PQube A fantastic modern callback to classic fixed-camera survival horror. It blends old-school tank controls and item-combining puzzles with modern graphics, set inside a creepy, mansion-like hospital.
Cry of Fear 2012 / Team Psyksallar A gritty, psychological source-engine mod turned standalone game. It depicts a young man's journey through a cold Scandinavian city, using claustrophobia and disturbing monster designs to represent mental illness.
Deadly Premonition 2010 / Access Games A quirky, janky, Twin Peaks-inspired open-world cult classic. It follows FBI Agent Francis York Morgan (and his split personality Zach) as they solve a serial killer mystery in a sleepy American town.
Overall Gaming Recommendation Play in a dark room with high-quality headphones to fully experience the rich soundscapes and atmospheric dread!

📼 Top 10 Favorite Horror & Psychological Movies

  1. [REC] (2007) — Intense, claustrophobic Spanish mockumentary that is one of the best found-footage films ever made.
  2. Lake Mungo (2008) — A haunting, melancholic mockumentary about grief, ghosts, and existential dread.
  3. Martyrs (2008) — An extremely heavy, thought-provoking French transgressive horror masterpiece on pain and transcendence.
  4. Perfect Blue (1997) — An unforgettable psychological thriller exploring identity, dread, and blurred reality.
  5. The Borderlands (2013) — Also known as Final Prayer, a slow-burn British found-footage film with a deeply disturbing ending.
  6. Grave Encounters (2011) — A fun, claustrophobic mockumentary exploring a haunted psychiatric hospital that warps space and time.
  7. Picnic (1996) — Shunji Iwai's short, dark, and beautifully whimsical psychological film about three patients walking on walls.
  8. Pulse (Kairo - 2001) — Kiyoshi Kurosawa's ultimate depiction of internet-dread and loneliness.
  9. Noroi: The Curse (2005) — A sprawling, deeply unsettling found-footage J-horror mystery.
  10. Cure (1997) — A hypnotic, philosophical J-horror mystery about suggestion, memory, and murder.
