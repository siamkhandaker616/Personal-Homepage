🌸 Categorized Activities & Interests

I believe in pursuing what truly inspires me. Here is how I divide my core activities, creative pursuits, and media interests. You can also explore my code repositories directly on my GitHub profile page to see my work.

🎮 Game Dev & Modding

  • Visual Novel / RPG Hybrids: My primary interest is to become a developer creating immersive VN/RPG hybrid games with rich branching narratives.
  • Engine Customization: Tinkering with retro engine files and writing custom styling code to adjust everything to my liking that I have recently started doing in The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim.

🦇 Retro Horror Gaming & Niche Movies

  • Atmospheric Classics: I love playing older, highly atmospheric survival horror video games like the original Silent Hill 2 and Obscure.
  • Janky Gems: I have a special place in my heart for quirky horror games that aren't technically polished or feature cheesy voice acting, like Deadly Premonition and Tormented Souls.
  • Niche Horror Movies: Exploring hidden indie horror gems and retro psychological thrillers from all over the world.

🖤 Anime & Visual Kei Music

  • Favorite Series: I live for stylish and dark anime like JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, Banana Fish, and Devilman.
  • Visual Kei: I am a huge fan of VK music, appreciating its unique fusion of theatrical rock, gothic fashion, and expressive melodies. My personal favorite bands include DAMNED, Xaa-Xaa, Kizu, lynch., DADAROMA, JILUKA, Versailles, DIAURA, and DazzlingBAD.

🌸 My Top 10 Lists

To give you a better idea of the specific niches I live for, here are my separate top ten lists for anime, survival horror video games, and psychological/indie horror movies:

🎬 Top 10 Favorite Anime Series & Movies

  1. Banana Fish — An absolute masterpiece street thriller with profound, deeply moving character bonds.
  2. JoJo's Bizarre Adventure — Stylized, theatrical action with iconic poses and musical references.
  3. Devilman Crybaby — A hyper-stylized, dark, tragic, and visually breathtaking modern masterpiece.
  4. Serial Experiments Lain — Deep cyber-psychology, digital dissociation, and dark philosophical atmosphere.
  5. Jujutsu Kaisen — Dynamic battles, dark occult themes, and stunning animations.
  6. Perfect Blue — Satoshi Kon's legendary psychological dread masterpiece mapping identity and obsession.
  7. Neon Genesis Evangelion — Masterful psychological struggles and iconic retro-mecha design.
  8. Mononoke — Avant-garde horror-fantasy with gorgeous theatrical art style.
  9. Texhnolyze — Gritty, bleak cyberpunk with intense existential dread.
  10. Monster — A dark, realistic psychological thriller about morality, guilt, and obsession.

Classic PS2 Era
(Golden Age) 		Silent Hill 2 Konami (2001) The absolute peak of psychological horror and melancholic atmosphere. Anytime I see foggy weather outsider I think of this game and despite the remake being out, I still honestly prefer the fixed-camera angle and the thrills only a PS2 game could provide.
Rule of Rose Sony (2006) A cruel, psychological gothic narrative set in a surreal orphanage ruled by children. I particularly love the eerie, dream-like atmosphere of the game and the hauntingly beautiful soundtrack it comes with.
Obscure Hydravision Entertainment (2004) A nostalgic and cheesy early 00's relic that captures almost everything I love from the soundtrack, to the characters and down to the atmosphere. I doubt most people will enjoy it since it has a lot of technical flaws as a product of its time, but I'd still very much recommend.
Fatal Frame II Tecmo (2003) Also known as Crimson Butterfly, this haunting J-horror classic features a camera-based combat system. It follows twin sisters lost in an abandoned village overrun by malevolent spirits, drawing on deep Japanese folklore.
Siren (Forbidden) Sony (2023) A highly atmospheric, challenging, multi-perspective stealth horror game. It requires players to 'sightjack' (see through the eyes of) the undead shibito to survive a perpetual red rain curse in a rural village.
Modern Retro &
Indie Gems 		Tormented Souls PQube (2021) A fantastic modern callback to classic fixed-camera survival horror. I love the limited ammo, lack of reliable save slots and zero hand-holding the game provides, forcing the player to really dig deep to figure things out.
Alan Wake Remedy Entertainment (2010) This game holds a special place in my heart with its storytelling, references to old Classics like The Twilight Zone and Twin peaks, along with the fantasic way in which it incorporated music and horror. A must play I'd say.
The Evil Within 2014 / Tango Gameworks Speaking of absurdly difficult games, I think even in its easiest difficulty this one is harder to beat than a lot of games out there. The repeated encounters with the same dreadfully powerful and scary bosses, claustrophic and disturbing imageries along with the soundtrack is sure to cause nightmares.
Fear
& Hunger		 Happy Paintings (2018) A modern day dungeon crawler that I think changed my brain chemistry. The visuals and the sources that inspired this game is one of my biggest inspirations for why I personally want to venture into game developement. The theatrical and frankly unspeakable suffering and misery that this game will make you go through is not for the faint of heart.
Deadly Premonition Access Games (2010) A quirky, janky, Twin Peaks-inspired open-world cult classic. I can probably only say bad things about the game but I swear I love it. It might just be so wrong that it is perfect.
Overall Gaming Recommendation Play in a dark room with high-quality headphones to fully experience the rich soundscapes and atmospheric dread!

📼 Top 10 Favorite Horror & Psychological Movies

  1. [REC] (2007) — Intense, claustrophobic Spanish mockumentary that is one of the best found-footage films ever made.
  2. Lake Mungo (2008) — A haunting, melancholic mockumentary about grief, ghosts, and existential dread.
  3. Martyrs (2008) — An extremely heavy, thought-provoking French transgressive horror masterpiece on pain and transcendence.
  4. The Borderlands (2013) — Also known as Final Prayer, a slow-burn British found-footage film with a deeply disturbing ending.
  5. Grave Encounters (2011) — A fun, claustrophobic mockumentary exploring a haunted psychiatric hospital that warps space and time.
  6. Picnic (1996) — Shunji Iwai's short, dark, and beautifully whimsical psychological film about three patients walking on walls.
  7. Pulse (Kairo - 2001) — Kiyoshi Kurosawa's ultimate depiction of internet-dread and loneliness.
  8. Noroi: The Curse (2005) — A sprawling, deeply unsettling found-footage J-horror mystery.
  9. Color Out of Space (2019) — A slowburn mystery that boils into a terrifying cosmic horror experience.
  10. Perfect Blue (1997) — An unforgettable psychological thriller exploring identity, dread, and blurred reality.
